The Pakistan Electric Power Company (PEPCO), a government-owned entity, was established on October 22, 1998, following a directive from the Prime Minister. It was incorporated as Pakistan Electric Power Company (Private) Limited under “With the Securities and Exchange Commission of Pakistan (SECP), in 2002. The Cabinet approved renaming Pakistan Electric Power Company (Private) Limited as “Power Planning and Monitoring Company (PPMC)” on June 21, 2024. Charged with carrying forward the mandate of planning and technical support to Ministry of Energy (Power Division), initially planning for the Power Sector of Pakistan, development and implementation of policy, CPM for power sector and monitoring, review and evaluate performance of Power Sector entities approved policies/plans.

Our Journey

1993
Strategic Foundation
The Cabinet Committee on Investment approved the Strategic Plan for the privatization of WAPDA Power Wing that included creation of an autonomous utility for WAPDA Power Wing privatization. This initiative laid the groundwork for structural reforms and the formation of sector institutions.
1998
Establishment of PEPCO
To implement the unbundling and support corporatization, Pakistan Electric Power Company (PEPCO) was formed. PEPCO assisted in smooth transition, Power Division monitoring and DISCO performance while ensuring sector continuity.
2021
Transformation to PPMC
Reflecting an expanded strategic role, PEPCO was rebranded as Power Planning and Monitoring Company (PPMC), aligning its identity with a broader mandate for integrated energy planning and policy support.
2021
NE Policy 2021
The Council of Common Interest approved the National Electricity Policy under Section 14(1) of the NEPRA Act, establishing it as the overarching framework for power sector development, reform and sustainability.
2023
NE Plan 2023–27
Pursuant to Section 14(4) of the National Electricity Plan was created to translate policy into operational guidelines, timelines and entity responsibilities, offering a five-year implementation perspective.
2025
Operational Launch
Following organizational redesign and process strengthening, PPMC became fully operational, equipped to deliver centralized planning, cross-sectoral coordination, climate fund coordination, and a future R&D roadmap for Pakistan's power sector.
